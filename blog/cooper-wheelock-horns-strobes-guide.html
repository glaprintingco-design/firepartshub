Overview

The Cooper Wheelock strobe series, including the MT Series, E70, and SpectrAlert, are pivotal in modern fire alarm systems. Their integration with protocols like CLIP (Classic Loop Interface Protocol), IDNET, and SLC (Signaling Line Circuit) ensures reliable performance. These devices meet stringent certifications such as UL 268 and FM, making them a trusted choice for fire safety. This guide explores the features and applications of these series, focusing on the cooper wheelock strobe and its role in comprehensive fire alarm systems.

Cooper Wheelock Strobe Technologies

Cooper Wheelock strobes are designed with versatility and compliance in mind. They are available in different configurations, addressing various installation needs, from wall-mounted to ceiling-mounted options. Their adaptability to Class A and Class B wiring configurations ensures they suit diverse infrastructure requirements.
Pro Tip:

When installing strobes like the 14N223 System Sensor SpectrAlert Wall Strobe, always check for compatibility with existing control panels to ensure seamless integration and avoid any synchronization issues.

MT Series

The Wheelock MT Series is renowned for its multi-tone capability, allowing it to be used in various signaling applications. This series supports multiple tones, which can be selected for specific alert types, enhancing the flexibility of your fire alarm system. The MT Series can be configured to work with both CLIP and SLC protocols, ensuring robust communication across the system.

E70 Series

The Wheelock E70 Series combines audible and visual alerts, making it an ideal choice for environments where both sound and light are necessary for effective signaling. The E70's sleek design allows for seamless integration into modern architectural spaces. It is compatible with IDNET protocol, enhancing its communicative reliability within sophisticated systems.
Pro Tip:

For optimal performance, ensure the strobe candela rating is appropriate for the environment size. Misjudging this can lead to inadequate coverage or excessive energy consumption.

SpectrAlert Series

The Wheelock SpectrAlert Series, including models like the System Sensor P2W Horn/Strobe and NEW SpectrAlert Advance System Sensor PC2R Ceiling Mount Horn Strobe ⚪️, represents the pinnacle of innovation in alarm signaling. These devices offer advanced performance with features such as field-selectable candela settings and compatibility with Class A and Class B wiring.

Installation Considerations

When replacing or installing Wheelock horn strobes, consider the following key factors to ensure compliance and performance: 1. **Wiring Configuration**: Determine whether Class A or Class B wiring is required. Class A provides redundancy, while Class B is simpler but without redundancy. 2. **Protocol Compatibility**: Ensure the device supports your system's protocol, whether it be CLIP, IDNET, or SLC. 3. **Environment**: Select devices with appropriate IP ratings for the installation environment to ensure durability and performance. 4. **Certification Compliance**: Verify that devices meet necessary certifications such as UL 268 for smoke detectors and UL 1971 for visual signaling devices.
